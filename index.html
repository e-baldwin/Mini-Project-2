Reflections

Data Selection: We chose the global COVID-19 dataset from Kaggle because it is a pandemic that has affected the lives of many around the world during this time. As a result, we wanted our project to have a practical application by illustrating the deadly results the pandemic has had, and whether there were any trends or significant correlation between a country's GDP, vaccination status, and age groups.

Before creating any visualizations with the dataset, we first had to determine which countries had no values for specific categories that we were analyzing. With these countries having NULL values, we opted to discard them to create a whole and complete dataset. Additionally, some of the data was mislabeled or incorrect. For instance, the total cases for 'North America' as a whole had been placed under the total cases per country.

Question 1: To express the total cases/total cases per million, we elected to use a treemap with the area representing the total cases and the shade of tcolor representing total cases per million. As expected, more populous countries yielded a greater number of cases, but that was not necessarily the case with cases per million.

In the same way, a treemap was also used to depict the total deaths/total deaths per million.

Question 3: It is known that the Corona Virus is more dangerous to older people and older populations. We displayed this data using side by side bar graphs in order to compare populations with people aged 65 and older with Covid-19 deaths. It can be seen that older populations tend to have higher death rates, however it is not as closely related as expected. We continue this discussion by using the median age of populations. We wanted to know that if a population had a higher median age, then they would also have a higher covid death rate. We used multiple bar charts to compare this data. We can see in this second graph that the covid-19 death rate is not correlated at all with an increased median age. This seems surprising. However, we decided to compare GDP with median age as well. GDP tends to increase with median age. We can understand that populations with older populations are more likely to have a higher GDP per capita. This is because countries with higher GDPs are more likely to have more health care related resources.
